Yo, what's up? Welcome to the **CInput** documentation! If you're looking to drop some modern graphical interfaces on your Casio fx-CG50 or ClassPad using Python and `gint`, you've found the holy grail.

CInput is your new best friend. It handles all the stressful stuff like on-screen keyboards, smooth scrollable lists, theming, and touch events, so you can focus on building legit add-ins without sweating the pixels. 

Whether you're crafting a dope physics solver or just a custom note-taking app, CInput makes your app look totally premium out of the box.

## Wait, Why CInput?

Because nobody wants to hardcode `dtext` coordinates all day! You just call `cinput.input()` and boom, an animated, themed on-screen keyboard drops in. You need the user to pick an option? `cinput.pick()` throws up a sick scrollable list. It's built for touch, it's fast, and it looks mad clean.
